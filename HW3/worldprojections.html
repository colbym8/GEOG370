World Map Projections

The following projections used the QGIS Indicatrix Mapping plugin to display
lines of latitude and longitude, as well as the depicted ellipsoids allowing for
shape and area comparisons. The vector layer used was from natural earth and was
altered using different projections found in the QGIS projection database. Each
projection selected altered the Indicatrix display to adjust to that projection.

Aitoff Azimuthal

This map shows an azimuthal Aitoff projection with equidistant measurements
that come at the expense of geographical shape and area. This equatorial
azimuthal projection shows the equator and prime meridian as normal lines
and ellipsoidal shape distortions at longitudes and latitudes of larger magnitudes.

WGS 84 Mercator

This map shows a WGS84 mercator projection in which the size and shape
become distorted at longitudes of higher magnitudes. This map creates line of
longitude and latitude that are parallel to one another as to create grid of
squares. Area and distances are both distorted.

WGS 84 Pseudo-Mercator

This is a pseudo mercator projection where geographic shape is maintained however
distance and size are distorted. The latitude and longitude lines are parallel to
each other and create a grid of rectangles that are elongated closer to the poles.

Sphere Winkel I

The sphere winkel map is a pseudo-cylindrical projection and attempts to minimize
distortions of distance, area, and shape all in the same map, however each
characteristic has minor distortions.

Equal Area Cylindrical

The world cylindrical projection is an equal area map in which land mass sizes are
proportionate to each other, however distances and shapes are distorted.

Equidistant Conical

The world equidistant conical map is a projection that accurately displays distance between
any two points on the map, however this proportionality doesn't apply to shape or area. This
projection is used for spatial analysis requiring accurate distance estimations using great circles.

North-Pole Azimuthal

The North-Pole azimuthal equidistant map is a projection where the north pole is the center
point from which all distances are proportional from point to point, very similar to the ESRI_54027
projection shown above.

Spherical Quartic Authalic

This spherical quartic authalic map is equal area, however shape and distance are distorted.
This is commonly used in Google Maps and OpenStreetMap.

Goode Homosoline

This projection attempts to maintain area and shape for landmasses with the exception of land
extremely close to the poles. Shapes and areas of oceans are distorted, as are areas for both
landmasses and bodies of water, showing accurate sizes and areas for inhabited regions.
