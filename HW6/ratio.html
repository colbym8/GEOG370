Employment Participation Percentage (Ages 20-24)

HW 6.2: Ratios & Choropleths

Equal Count Scale

This map uses an equal count scale, meaning the 254 counties in the state of
Texas are divided evenly into five different classes. Equal count categorization
creates a map with equal representation of the five classes. However, it can skew
how the data is perceived in the case of outlier counties being grouped with
counties of much larger or smaller magnitudes. Another limitation could be two
counties with very similar employment participation values being placed in two different
classes because they fall at the minimum value of one class and the maximum value
of another.

Equal Interval Scale

This map uses an equal interval scale, meaning the five classes are broken into five
equal intervals, in this case five intervals of 20% each. This categorization can
be useful when showing data that has a large range, however more than half of the
counties employment participation percentage lies in the 60%-80% class, which doesn't
allow for differentiation between the counties that are closer to 60% participation
and those that are closer to 80%. The equal interval scale shows that the equal
count scale has more drawbacks for this specfic data set, being that 80% of Texas
counties have an employment participation rate of at least 64% (based on the logic
that each class has an equal amount and four of the five classes have a rate of 64%
or greater), however the equal count scale represents counties that might have a
63% rate and another with an 81% rate as being 4 classes apart from one another.

Natural Breaks Scale

This map uses a natural breaks scale, meaning the five classes are broken based
on where natural gaps or significant margins exist between clusters of county
participation values. Natural break categorization is the most fitting for this
(and many other) data sets, because it allows the viewer to understand that many
Texas counties have similar employment participation rates among the 20-24 age group,
while more clearly identifying outlier groups. This natural breaks map has a similar
appearance to the equal interval map due to the nature of the data clustering around
the 60%-80% range, but it is able to more precisely categorize the data in the 0%-8%
class (8% is more precise than 20%) and in the 82% to 100% class (18% is more precise
thank 20%).
