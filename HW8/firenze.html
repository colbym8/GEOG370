Florence Transportation Network

"How has the government in Florence and Tuscany built transportation infrastructure
to connect the population centers of the region?" and "How will I navigate Tuscany
when I'm studying abroad in Florence this fall?"

I'm an environmental science major studying smart cities and regional planning
(possibly with a concentration on transportation) and I'm generally interested in
transportation systems, Italian culture, and traveling. This is going to be my
first experience outside of the United States, so I wanted to make a map featuring
the different transportation lines in the Florence Metro Area, including major
roads, railways, biking paths, and walking paths. Hopefully my three semesters of
taking Italian will pay off when I'm trying to understand these transportation lines
this fall.



This map shows all of the different transportation systems mentioned above, combined
in one layer as a transportation 'network'. I also displayed the cities and towns,
with their population ranges, in the region surrounding Florence.
I used five different vector layers to create one (1)Union layer that incorporated all
of the systems into the network shown on the map. Then I used (2)Trim to create layers
of Italian cities and Italian transportation lines that existed only inside the Florence
metro area boundaries.
To create the reference/location map in the top right corner,
I also used (3)Dissolve to create a polygon layer without the internal political boundaries
the shapefile I downloaded originally had, creating an outline of Italy (because I was
somehow struggling to find an outline).
